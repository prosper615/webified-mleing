Influenced by reading the summary of paper on message lock encryption by Ananth Raghunathan, Dan Boneh, Ilya Mironove and co. Message lock encryption is just like deriving the encryption key from the message itself . This means the same message equals the same key and the same ciphertext and also vice versa. It enables storage systems to detect and eliminate duplicates
You could try putting the same message two times to see how this works
